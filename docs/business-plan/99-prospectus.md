## The problem

Enterprises and governments are racing to put AI on their data but **cannot prove governance to a regulator**.
Useful AI needs business context (definitions, lineage, quality); regulated organizations additionally need
every AI access to be **governed, sovereign, and recorded in a tamper-evident audit trail**. Today's catalog
and AI-governance tools deliver context but treat audit, isolation, and sovereignty as afterthoughts. **AI is
scaling faster than compliance.**

## The solution & moat

Doxa is a compliance-grade **data & AI governance platform** — catalog, lineage, policy, and "context for AI" —
built on a trust spine competitors must retrofit:

1. **Immutable, SHA-256-signed WORM audit ledger** — trace every AI answer to a record you can hand an auditor.
2. **Data sovereignty / air-gap** — wins government and the highest-sensitivity enterprises.
3. **Database-per-tenant isolation + cryptographic-shred certified offboarding.**
4. **Control-mapped by construction** — SOC 2 (CC6/CC7), HIPAA (§164.312), NIST 800-53 (AU).

These are not roadmap aspirations — they are documented, production-grade architecture in the company today,
which is the hardest part to retrofit for regulated buyers. The raise funds the **product** on top of this spine.
